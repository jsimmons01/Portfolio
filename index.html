My Story

How does a teacher become a developer? My story is very simple, I have always had an interest in programming. It started in 2009 when I took intro to computer science in college. I learned Java it was my 1st time being introduced to coding. I really enjoyed doing the projects, but since it didn’t come naturally to me like it did for other students, I thought I wasn’t good and didn’t pursue it further.

In 2020 I did a coding workshop where I learned to build a static landing page with HTML, CSS, and JavaScript. I really enjoyed it. This reignited my interest in coding and since then I have been improving my skills in object oriented programming and web development. So to answer the question how does a teacher become a web developer, is quite simple. My passion was lying doormat because I had the wrong impression.

After taking the coding workshop I spent a few months self-teaching because I decided I wanted to pursue this career path. I joined Nucamp Bootcamp so I can have structure and put my best foot forward. I spent at least 4 hours everyday most times more, even on weekends coding, practicing, and learning. I have definitely grown as a developer since the boot camp. Some of the difficult concepts that I didn’t understand while taking the boot camp it makes sense know and comes easy.

I am now seeking a full-time role where I can contribute my skills in web development and use my transferable soft skills as a teacher (effective communication, continous learning, problem solving) to help a compay achieve their goals.
